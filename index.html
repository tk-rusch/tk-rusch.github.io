T. Konstantin Rusch

Postdoctoral researcher at MIT.

My name is T. Konstantin Rusch and I’m a postdoctoral researcher at CSAIL, MIT advised by Daniela Rus. Before that I finished a PhD in Machine Learning / Applied Mathematics at ETH Zurich, advised by Siddhartha Mishra. Moreover, during my PhD studies I had a second affiliation at the EECS Department at UC Berkeley, advised by Michael Mahoney.


I am a computational applied mathematician focused on AI research. Much of my work takes inspirations from physics and neuroscience to develop novel state-of-the-art machine learning approaches. My research is deeply grounded in computational science and statistics, providing rigorous mathematical insights. My applications primarily focus on the natural sciences and robotics, with a particular emphasis on physical intelligence.
